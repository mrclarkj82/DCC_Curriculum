# B-Roll Sequence

- Project ID: vp-p02
- Program Area ID: video-production
- Program Area: Video Production Studio
- Project Type: b-roll-sequence
- Status: pilot-ready, needs teacher review

## Student-Facing Project Overview

Students capture or collect a short sequence of B-roll clips and submit a simple edited sequence or media evidence according to teacher requirements.

## Production Goal

Create a short B-roll sequence that uses shot variety to show a subject, activity, place, or process clearly.

## Required Steps

1. Choose a teacher-approved subject or activity.
2. Capture a variety of shots such as wide, medium, close-up, detail, movement, and reaction shots.
3. Organize clips in an external editing tool selected by the teacher or school.
4. Export or link the sequence using the naming convention.
5. Submit the media evidence and reflection.

## Equipment/Software Notes

- Teacher-selected camera, phone, school camera, or existing media source as appropriate
- External editing tool selected by the teacher or school if editing is required
- DCC Creative Studio will host and organize submissions later; it will not edit video

## Shot List or Production Checklist

- Wide shot
- Medium shot
- Close-up or detail shot
- At least one intentional camera movement or angle change
- Exported sequence or organized clip evidence
- Reflection

## Submission Requirements

- Exported B-roll sequence or hosted link
- Shot list or production notes
- Short reflection

## Acceptable File/Link Types

- video
- link
- fileUpload
- textReflection

## File Naming Expectations

Use `LastName_FirstName_ProjectID_Version`, such as `LastName_FirstName_VP-P02_v1` or `LastName_FirstName_VP-P02_Final`.

## 4-Point Rubric

- **4:** Complete, polished, on time, and clearly meets or extends the stated requirements.
- **3:** Complete and meets the stated requirements.
- **2:** Partially complete or missing required evidence.
- **1:** Attempted but incomplete, unclear, or not functional.
- **0:** Not submitted.

## Reflection Prompt

Which shot best helped tell the story, and why?

## Common Problems

- Students submit one long clip instead of a sequence.
- Shots are too similar to show variety.
- Audio or background distractions pull attention from the visual subject.
- Exported files are missing names or versions.

## Teacher Notes

Keep the first B-roll pilot small. The goal is to test instructions, evidence collection, naming, and reflection before building a larger production workflow.

## Important Boundary

The website hosts, collects, organizes, and reviews video production work. The website does not edit video.
