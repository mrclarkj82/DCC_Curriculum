# Assignment Sheet: B-Roll Sequence

## Objective

Create a short B-roll sequence that uses shot variety to show a subject, activity, place, or process clearly.

## Skill Focus

- shot variety
- sequencing
- visual storytelling
- media export

## Required Steps

1. Choose a teacher-approved subject or activity.
2. Capture a variety of shots such as wide, medium, close-up, detail, movement, and reaction shots.
3. Organize clips in an external editing tool selected by the teacher or school.
4. Export or link the sequence using the naming convention.
5. Submit the media evidence and reflection.

## Naming Convention

Use `LastName_FirstName_ProjectID_Version`. Example: `LastName_FirstName_VP-P02_v1`.

## Screenshot/Video/File Evidence Requirements

- Exported B-roll sequence or hosted link
- Shot list or production notes
- Short reflection

## What To Do If Stuck

- Re-read the teacher-created project or Broadcast Desk Update instructions.
- Check file sharing permissions before submitting links.
- Ask which evidence item is missing instead of asking whether the whole project is done.
- Remember that editing happens in external tools selected by the teacher or school.

## 4-Point Rubric

- **4:** Complete, polished, on time, and clearly meets or extends the stated requirements.
- **3:** Complete and meets the stated requirements.
- **2:** Partially complete or missing required evidence.
- **1:** Attempted but incomplete, unclear, or not functional.
- **0:** Not submitted.

## Extension Challenge

Add one extra production note explaining a creative decision you made.

## Reflection Prompt

Which shot best helped tell the story, and why?
