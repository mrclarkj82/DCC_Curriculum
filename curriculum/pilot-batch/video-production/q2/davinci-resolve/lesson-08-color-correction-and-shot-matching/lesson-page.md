## Vocabulary

- Color page: The Resolve page used for color correction and grading.
- Color management: Settings that help convert footage from the camera color space into a useful editing and viewing space.
- Input color space: The camera or recording color profile assigned to footage so Resolve interprets it correctly.
- Node: A container for color corrections that can be enabled, disabled, reset, or organized in order.
- Lift: A primary color wheel control that mostly affects darker parts of the image.
- Gamma: A primary color wheel control that mostly affects midtones.
- Gain: A primary color wheel control that mostly affects brighter parts of the image.
- Saturation: The strength or intensity of color in the image.
- White balance: Adjusting color temperature and tint so neutral areas look natural.

## Common Problems and Fixes

- Students may treat color correction as random filters instead of controlled adjustment.
- Students may over-saturate footage and lose natural skin tones or detail.
- Students may skip before/after evidence and rely only on taste.
