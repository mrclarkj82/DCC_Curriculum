## Vocabulary

- Deliver page: The Resolve page used to render or export a timeline into a video file.
- Render: To convert the timeline into a finished video file.
- Codec: A method for compressing and storing video or audio data.
- H.264: A common delivery codec that is widely compatible for online video.
- H.265: A newer efficient codec that can make smaller high-quality files but may have compatibility limits.
- ProRes: A high-quality editing and mastering codec that usually creates larger files.
- Render queue: The list of export jobs waiting to render in Resolve.
- Single clip export: Rendering the whole timeline as one video file.

## Common Problems and Fixes

- Students may think the Resolve project file is the same thing as the exported movie.
- Students may forget to add the job to the render queue before rendering.
- Students may use unclear file names that make final submissions hard to identify.

## Student-Facing Help

- Save the Resolve project before switching pages or exporting.
- Keep evidence links in Google Drive, Google Docs, or YouTube according to teacher directions.
- Use the class naming convention for screenshots, exports, and written evidence.
- Do not upload raw files directly to the website.
