## Vocabulary

- Trim: To shorten or extend a clip by adjusting where it starts or ends.
- In point: The selected start of the part of a source clip that will be used.
- Out point: The selected end of the part of a source clip that will be used.
- Rough cut: An early version of an edit focused on order, timing, and story instead of polish.
- Insert edit: An edit that places a clip and pushes later material down the timeline.
- Ripple trim: A trim that closes or opens timeline space so clips stay connected.
- Blade or split: A cut that divides one clip into separate pieces.

- Students may add every clip without selecting the best part.
- Students may leave accidental gaps and think the edit is finished.
- Students may overuse transitions before the basic cut works.
