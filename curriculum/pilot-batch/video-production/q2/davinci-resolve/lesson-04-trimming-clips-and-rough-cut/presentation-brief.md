# Presentation Brief: Trimming Clips and Building a Rough Cut

This premium brief is for creating a 15-20 slide teacher-led PowerPoint. It is intentionally deeper than the 9-slide automation brief.

## Lesson Overview

- Program area: Video Production Studio
- Lesson ID: vp-q2-l04
- Quarter: Q2
- Unit: DaVinci Resolve Foundations
- Class length: 90 minutes
- Learning target: I can trim clips, set in and out points, and build a rough cut that communicates a simple story without unnecessary dead space.
- Bell ringer: What makes a video clip feel too long, even if the shot itself is useful?
- Assignment: Rough Cut Story Sequence
- Video transcript range: 00:35:30-01:07:04
- Timestamp note: Timestamp range is based on the stored DaVinci Resolve tutorial transcript. Teacher may trim the demo if class time is tight.

## Big Idea

Video editing is not one magic button. It is a sequence of organized post-production passes: media, edit, basic audio checks, graphics, and delivery.

## Classroom Hook

Show a messy folder of random clips next to a clean project timeline. Ask which one would be easier to finish under deadline and why.

## Student-Friendly Analogy

Editing is like building a school broadcast segment: collect the ingredients, sort them, assemble the story, polish sound and image, then deliver a version others can watch.

## Why This Matters For Digital Content Creators

Students who can manage footage, make clean edits, and export correctly can contribute to broadcasts, social media packages, interviews, event recaps, and later group projects.

- Sign in to the classroom portal and locate the active Today item.
- Open DaVinci Resolve on the assigned computer.
- Use Google Drive, Google Docs, or YouTube links for evidence submission.
- trimming

- keyboard shortcut practice

## Misconception Warnings

- Students may add every clip without selecting the best part.
- Students may leave accidental gaps and think the edit is finished.
- Students may overuse transitions before the basic cut works.

## Teacher Demo Sequence

1. Open the timeline from Lesson 03.
2. Trim the beginning and end of clips to remove dead space, slates, or unusable moments.
3. Use in and out points on at least two source clips before adding them to the timeline.
4. Build a 30-60 second rough cut with a clear order.
5. Remove unintended gaps unless there is an intentional pause.
6. Capture evidence of the rough cut timeline and write a short editing rationale.

## Suggested 15-20 Slide Structure

1. Title and learning target: establish the workflow and evidence goal.
2. Bell ringer: What makes a video clip feel too long, even if the shot itself is useful?
3. Big idea: Resolve work happens in passes, not all at once.
4. Why creators care: connect the workflow to broadcast, YouTube, interviews, or client-style work.
5. Transcript anchor: use 00:35:30-01:07:04 from the stored tutorial source.
6. Key vocabulary: Trim, In point, Out point, Rough cut, Insert edit, Ripple trim.
7. Interface map: show the Resolve page, panel, or tool students need today.
8. Teacher demo part 1: model the first half of the workflow slowly.
9. Teacher demo part 2: model the evidence or quality check.
10. Common mistakes: show one broken example and one fixed example.
11. Guided checkpoint: students point to the correct panel/tool before independent work.
12. Assignment launch: Rough Cut Story Sequence.
13. Evidence checklist: show exactly what link-based evidence is required.
14. Work time checkpoint 1: teacher checks setup and naming.
15. Work time checkpoint 2: teacher checks the visible Resolve result.
16. Extension challenge: Create two versions of the same rough cut with different pacing: one calm and one energetic.
17. Studio challenge: Use only trimming and clip order to make the story clearer before adding titles, effects, or music.
18. Reflection and exit ticket: Name one trimming tool or shortcut you used today and explain what it changed in your rough cut.
