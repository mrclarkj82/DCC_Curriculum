## Vocabulary

- Foliage: Trees, grass, plants, and similar environment detail placed across a scene.
- Prop: A placed object that adds detail, function, or story to a scene.
- Environment pass: A focused improvement pass that adds believable detail to an environment.
- Player path: The route a player is encouraged to follow through a space.
- Focal point: The area the viewer or player should notice first.
- Density: How many foliage instances appear in an area.
- Variation: Differences in scale, rotation, color, or placement that make repeated assets feel natural.
- Blueprint: A visual scripting asset used to create behavior or interaction in Unreal.
- Trigger: A volume or event that starts an action when something overlaps or activates it.
- Playable space: An area designed to be explored or used by a player.

## Common Problems and Fixes

- Students paint foliage too densely and hide the intended path.
- Students forget to check scale against a character or familiar object.
- Students add props randomly instead of supporting a focal point.
- Students confuse a Blueprint concept with a required full game system.
- Students submit a pretty screenshot without showing close-up detail or interaction plan.

## Student-Facing Help

- Use density carefully; empty space can help the player read the path.
- Use variation in scale, rotation, and placement so repeated assets feel less copied.
- If you cannot build the Blueprint preview, describe the trigger or interaction clearly.
- Play or camera test the scene before final screenshots.
