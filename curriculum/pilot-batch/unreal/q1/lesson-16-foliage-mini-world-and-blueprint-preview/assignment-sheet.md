# Mini-World Environment Pass Assignment Sheet

## Objective

I can add environmental detail to a small Unreal scene and explain how foliage, props, and simple interaction prepare a scene for a playable mini-world.

## Skill Focus

- foliage mode setup
- density and scale control
- mini-world composition
- player path or focal area
- Blueprint preview concept

## Required Steps

1. Open the landscape or small scene from the previous lesson.
2. Add foliage or environmental props using Foliage mode, placed static meshes, or teacher-approved assets.
3. Adjust density, scale, or placement so the scene is detailed but readable.
4. Create or clarify a player path, focal area, or mini-world destination.
5. Include a simple Blueprint preview screenshot or written interaction concept if your setup supports it.
6. Play test or camera test the mini-world from a student/player viewpoint.
7. Upload evidence screenshots to Google Drive and paste links into DCC Creative Studio.
8. Write a reflection explaining how the scene became more believable or playable.

## Naming Convention

Use this format when naming screenshots or exported evidence: LastName_FirstName_UE-Q1-A16_Description.

## Google Drive Link Evidence Requirements

Upload screenshots, short screen recordings, or written evidence to Google Drive. Paste share links into DCC Creative Studio. Do not upload raw files directly to the website.

- Google Drive link to a wide screenshot of mini-world environment.
- Google Drive link to a close-up screenshot showing foliage or prop detail.
- Google Drive link to a screenshot or written description of simple interaction or Blueprint preview.
- Short reflection explaining how the scene became more believable or playable.

## What To Do If Stuck

- Use density carefully; empty space can help the player read the path.
- Use variation in scale, rotation, and placement so repeated assets feel less copied.
- If you cannot build the Blueprint preview, describe the trigger or interaction clearly.
- Play or camera test the scene before final screenshots.

## 4-Point Rubric

- 4: Complete, polished, on time, and clearly meets or extends the stated requirements.
- 3: Complete and meets the stated requirements.
- 2: Partially complete or missing required evidence.
- 1: Attempted but incomplete, unclear, or not functional.
- 0: Not submitted.

## Extension Challenge

Add one optional interaction concept, such as a trigger area, checkpoint, collectable, or simple win condition, and describe how it would work.

## Studio Challenge

Design the mini-world so a player can tell where to go within five seconds of looking at the scene.

## Reflection Prompt

Explain how foliage, props, path design, or Blueprint preview changed your scene from a terrain study into a mini-world.
