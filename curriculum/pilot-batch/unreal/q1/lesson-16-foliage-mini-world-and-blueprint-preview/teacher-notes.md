## Teacher Mini-Lesson Talking Points

- Start from a simple landscape or scene with a clear focal area.
- Add foliage assets to Foliage mode and show paint density.
- Fix over-dense foliage by lowering density or erasing.
- Check scale using a mannequin, player start, or familiar prop.
- Move individual instances when a painted result needs artistic adjustment.
- Point out the player path and what the viewer should notice first.
- Preview a simple Blueprint idea such as trigger, overlap, or win message without requiring a full game build.

## Likely Student Issues

- Students paint foliage too densely and hide the intended path.
- Students forget to check scale against a character or familiar object.
- Students add props randomly instead of supporting a focal point.
- Students confuse a Blueprint concept with a required full game system.
- Students submit a pretty screenshot without showing close-up detail or interaction plan.

## Intervention Ideas

- Ask students to point to the exact setting, object, or screenshot that proves the skill.
- Pair students for a 2-minute evidence check before submission.
- Have students reset to a simpler scene if visual clutter hides the target skill.
- For students who finish early, move them into the extension or studio challenge rather than unrelated features.

## Extension Ideas

Add one optional interaction concept, such as a trigger area, checkpoint, collectable, or simple win condition, and describe how it would work.

## Studio Challenge

Design the mini-world so a player can tell where to go within five seconds of looking at the scene.

## Vocabulary Emphasis

- Foliage: Trees, grass, plants, and similar environment detail placed across a scene.
- Prop: A placed object that adds detail, function, or story to a scene.
- Environment pass: A focused improvement pass that adds believable detail to an environment.
- Player path: The route a player is encouraged to follow through a space.
- Focal point: The area the viewer or player should notice first.
- Density: How many foliage instances appear in an area.
- Variation: Differences in scale, rotation, color, or placement that make repeated assets feel natural.
- Blueprint: A visual scripting asset used to create behavior or interaction in Unreal.
- Trigger: A volume or event that starts an action when something overlaps or activates it.
- Playable space: An area designed to be explored or used by a player.
