This premium brief is for creating a 15-20 slide teacher-led PowerPoint. It is intentionally deeper than the 9-slide automation brief.

## Big Idea

A believable environment needs intentional detail, readable paths, and at least a first idea of interaction.

## Classroom Hook

Show a bare terrain next to the same terrain with path-side grass, trees, rocks, and a clear destination. Ask which one feels playable.

## Student-Friendly Analogy

A mini-world is like a movie set and a game level at the same time: props create the look, but paths and triggers suggest what a player can do.

## Why This Matters For Digital Content Creators

Digital content creators build spaces that people can read, explore, film, or play. Foliage and props are not decoration only; they guide attention and story.

## Prerequisite Skills

- Open and navigate an Unreal project.
- Use the Content Drawer and Outliner without losing project organization.
- Capture screenshots that clearly show evidence.
- Upload screenshots or written evidence to Google Drive and paste share links into DCC Creative Studio.
- foliage mode setup
- density and scale control
- mini-world composition
- player path or focal area

## Misconception Warnings

- More foliage is not automatically better.
- A Blueprint preview is a concept checkpoint, not a full programming unit today.
- A mini-world should guide the viewer; it should not become visual clutter.

## Teacher Demo Sequence

1. Start from a simple landscape or scene with a clear focal area.
2. Add foliage assets to Foliage mode and show paint density.
3. Fix over-dense foliage by lowering density or erasing.
4. Check scale using a mannequin, player start, or familiar prop.
5. Move individual instances when a painted result needs artistic adjustment.
6. Point out the player path and what the viewer should notice first.
7. Preview a simple Blueprint idea such as trigger, overlap, or win message without requiring a full game build.
