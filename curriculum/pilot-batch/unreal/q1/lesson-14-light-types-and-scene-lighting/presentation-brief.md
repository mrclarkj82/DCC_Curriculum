## Big Idea

Lighting tools are creative choices. The type of light, not just its brightness, changes where the viewer looks and how the scene feels.

## Classroom Hook

Show the same object lit like a flashlight, a sunset, a stage, and a bright classroom. Ask students which version tells the clearest story.

## Student-Friendly Analogy

Think of a school theater: a ceiling bulb, stage spotlight, window light, sun direction, and room fill all shape the same space differently.

## Why This Matters For Digital Content Creators

Game artists, filmmakers, stream designers, and virtual production teams use lighting to guide attention and create mood before a viewer reads any text.

## Prerequisite Skills

- Open and navigate an Unreal project.
- Use the Content Drawer and Outliner without losing project organization.
- Capture screenshots that clearly show evidence.
- Upload screenshots or written evidence to Google Drive and paste share links into DCC Creative Studio.
- point light setup
- spotlight or rectangle light control
- directional light and skylight awareness
- mood and focal point

## Misconception Warnings

- More lights do not automatically make a better scene.
- Mood is not only color; shadow direction and softness matter too.
- Skylight is environmental fill, not the same as a visible sun beam.

## Teacher Demo Sequence

1. Start with a simple prop or room and no extra lights.
2. Drag in a point light and adjust intensity and source radius.
3. Replace or add a spotlight; rotate it and change cone settings.
4. Show a rectangle light as a soft panel or window-like source.
5. Add or adjust directional light and skylight for outdoor or environmental light.
6. Compare mood words: harsh, soft, warm, cold, dramatic, calm.
7. Model screenshot naming and reflection language.

## Independent Work Expectations

- Open a small scene with at least one object, wall, prop, or focal area.
- Create a first lighting setup using a point light.
- Create a second lighting setup using a spotlight or rectangle light.
- Create a third lighting setup using a directional light, skylight, or environment lighting.
- Adjust intensity, color temperature, color, angle, or shadow softness intentionally.
- Capture one screenshot for each lighting setup.
- Upload screenshots to Google Drive and paste share links into DCC Creative Studio.
- Write a reflection explaining which setup created the strongest mood and why.
