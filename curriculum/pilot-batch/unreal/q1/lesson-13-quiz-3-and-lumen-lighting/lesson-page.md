## Vocabulary

- Lumen: Unreal Engine 5 system for dynamic global illumination and reflections.
- Global illumination: Light that bounces around a scene instead of stopping after one hit.
- Reflection: Light or scene information that appears on a shiny surface.
- Indirect light: Light that reaches an area after bouncing from another surface.
- Real-time lighting: Lighting that updates while objects or lights move.
- Exposure: Camera brightness control that changes how light or dark a scene appears.
- Shadow: A darker area created when light is blocked.
- Lighting evidence: Screenshots or notes that prove how lighting changed the scene.
- Light map: A baked texture that stores static lighting information.
- Movable light: A light that can update dynamically instead of relying on baked lighting.

## Common Problems and Fixes

- Students treat the lesson as only a quiz day and do not create lighting evidence.
- Students move a light but do not capture before and after screenshots from the same camera angle.
- Students confuse exposure brightness with true bounced light.
- Students forget that static baked lighting can break when objects or lights move.
- Students submit screenshots that are too dark to prove the lighting change.

## Student-Facing Help

- Use the same camera angle for before and after screenshots.
- Look for shadow fill, color bounce, softer shadow edges, or reflected light.
- If your scene is too dark, check exposure before deciding that Lumen is broken.
- Use vocabulary such as global illumination, indirect light, and bounce light in your reflection.
