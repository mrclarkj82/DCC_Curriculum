# Lumen Lighting Comparison Assignment Sheet

## Objective

I can explain the basic purpose of Lumen and use lighting evidence to show how global illumination changes an Unreal scene.

## Skill Focus

- Quiz 3 opening checkpoint
- Lumen purpose
- global illumination evidence
- before and after lighting comparison
- lighting reflection language

## Required Steps

1. Complete or review Quiz 3 using the teacher-approved workflow.
2. Open a simple lighting test scene, migrated lighting example, or teacher-provided scene.
3. Capture a before screenshot where lighting looks flat, too dark, or not yet improved.
4. Identify where Lumen or global illumination settings affect the scene.
5. Adjust the lighting setup, movable light, source radius, exposure, or Lumen-related setting as directed.
6. Capture an improved screenshot showing clearer bounce light, softer shadow, or more believable lighting.
7. Upload evidence screenshots to Google Drive and paste share links into DCC Creative Studio.
8. Write a short reflection explaining how lighting changed the scene.

## Naming Convention

Use this format when naming screenshots or exported evidence: LastName_FirstName_UE-Q1-A13_Description.

## Google Drive Link Evidence Requirements

Upload screenshots, short screen recordings, or written evidence to Google Drive. Paste share links into DCC Creative Studio. Do not upload raw files directly to the website.

- Quiz 3 completion record or teacher checkoff.
- Google Drive link to a screenshot showing a lighting setup before adjustment.
- Google Drive link to a screenshot showing improved Lumen or global illumination result.
- Short reflection explaining how lighting changed the scene.

## What To Do If Stuck

- Use the same camera angle for before and after screenshots.
- Look for shadow fill, color bounce, softer shadow edges, or reflected light.
- If your scene is too dark, check exposure before deciding that Lumen is broken.
- Use vocabulary such as global illumination, indirect light, and bounce light in your reflection.

## 4-Point Rubric

- 4: Complete, polished, on time, and clearly meets or extends the stated requirements.
- 3: Complete and meets the stated requirements.
- 2: Partially complete or missing required evidence.
- 1: Attempted but incomplete, unclear, or not functional.
- 0: Not submitted.

## Extension Challenge

Create a three-image lighting comparison: no Lumen or weak bounce light, improved Lumen/global illumination, and a dramatic mood version.

## Studio Challenge

Use one colored surface or emissive-looking material to create visible color bounce or mood lighting evidence.

## Reflection Prompt

Explain how Lumen or global illumination changed the mood, realism, or readability of your scene.
