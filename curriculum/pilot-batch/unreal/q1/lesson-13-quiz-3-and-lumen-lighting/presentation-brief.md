## Big Idea

Realistic lighting is not just brightness. It is how light bounces, fills shadows, reflects, and helps the viewer understand the scene.

## Classroom Hook

Show two screenshots of the same simple room: one with dead black shadows and one with believable bounced light. Ask which one feels more real and why.

## Student-Friendly Analogy

Lumen is like light in a real room: a red wall can make nearby shadows feel slightly red because light bounces instead of stopping.

## Why This Matters For Digital Content Creators

Digital content creators use lighting evidence to make game spaces, cinematic shots, product renders, and portfolio screenshots feel intentional instead of flat.

- Open and navigate an Unreal project.
- Use the Content Drawer and Outliner without losing project organization.
- Capture screenshots that clearly show evidence.
- Upload screenshots or written evidence to Google Drive and paste share links into DCC Creative Studio.
- Quiz 3 opening checkpoint

## Misconception Warnings

- Brighter does not always mean better lighting.
- Lumen is not a magic beautify button; students still need a readable light setup.
- A before and after comparison only works when the camera angle is controlled.

## Teacher Demo Sequence

1. Frame Quiz 3 as a quick checkpoint, not the whole class period.
2. Show a simple room or test scene with obvious dark shadows.
3. Explain global illumination as light bounce and color bleed.
4. Compare baked/static lighting with movable lighting behavior.
5. Toggle or compare Lumen-style dynamic lighting evidence.
6. Move a light or object and point out how the lighting responds.
7. Adjust source radius or exposure only after students know what evidence to capture.
