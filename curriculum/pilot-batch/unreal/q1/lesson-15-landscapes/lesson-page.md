## Vocabulary

- Landscape: A terrain surface used to build outdoor ground in Unreal.
- Terrain: The shape and surface of outdoor ground such as hills, valleys, and cliffs.
- Sculpt: A landscape tool action that raises or lowers terrain.
- Smooth: A tool that softens sharp or bumpy terrain changes.
- Flatten: A tool that creates a more level area from a chosen height.
- Erosion: A natural wearing-down effect or terrain idea used to make landforms feel less artificial.
- Elevation: The height of terrain compared with nearby areas.
- Brush size: How wide the landscape tool affects the terrain.
- Brush strength: How strongly the tool changes terrain.
- Scale: How large the terrain feels compared with the player or objects.

## Assignment Summary

Create a landscape, sculpt at least three elevation zones, smooth or refine terrain, and frame a useful screenshot of the result.

## Submission Checklist

- Google Drive link to a screenshot of Landscape mode or landscape setup.
- Google Drive link to a wide shot of sculpted terrain.
- Google Drive link to a close-up showing terrain shaping or refinement.
- Short reflection naming the landscape tools used.

## Extension Challenge

Add a clear path, overlook, valley, or buildable platform that could support a future mini-world.

## Exit Ticket

Which landscape tool changed your terrain most clearly, and how?

## Common Problems and Fixes

- Students leave the landscape almost flat.
- Students sculpt spikes instead of believable hills or playable forms.
- Students forget to smooth or refine harsh edges.
- Students make terrain too large or too small to understand from screenshots.
- Students submit only a close-up and no wide shot.

## Student-Facing Help

- Start broad first; use large brush sizes for big forms before small details.
- Use Shift with sculpting when you need to lower terrain.
- Use Smooth after extreme sculpting to remove harsh artifacts.
- Use Flatten to create a level area for a player path, structure, or focal object.
