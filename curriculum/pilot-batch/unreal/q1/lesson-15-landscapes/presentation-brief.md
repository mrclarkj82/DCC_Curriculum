# Presentation Brief: Landscapes

This premium brief is for creating a 15-20 slide teacher-led PowerPoint. It is intentionally deeper than the 9-slide automation brief.

## Big Idea

A strong environment starts with readable large shapes before small props, foliage, or decorations are added.

## Classroom Hook

Show a flat plane beside a simple hill, valley, and platform. Ask which one already suggests a scene or route.

## Student-Friendly Analogy

Landscape sculpting is like shaping clay: first block out the big form, then smooth and refine the places where people will look or walk.

## Why This Matters For Digital Content Creators

Terrain affects composition, player movement, screenshot quality, and whether a digital world feels designed or random.

## Prerequisite Skills

- Open and navigate an Unreal project.
- Use the Content Drawer and Outliner without losing project organization.
- Capture screenshots that clearly show evidence.
- Upload screenshots or written evidence to Google Drive and paste share links into DCC Creative Studio.
- landscape creation
- terrain sculpting
- smooth and flatten tools
- elevation zones

## Misconception Warnings

- Natural terrain is not the same as random spikes.
- Small details cannot fix a weak large terrain shape.
- Flatten is not only for making everything flat; it can create purposeful playable areas.

## Teacher Demo Sequence

1. Create or open a blank map and enter Landscape mode.
2. Create the landscape using a manageable default setup.
3. Show sculpting to raise terrain and Shift sculpting to lower it.
4. Change brush size and strength so students see control levels.
5. Use Smooth to repair a harsh sculpt.
6. Use Flatten to create a terrace, path, or playable platform.
7. Frame screenshots that prove wide form and close-up refinement.

## Independent Work Expectations

- Create or open a clean Unreal map for the terrain study.
- Enter Landscape mode and create a basic landscape.
- Sculpt at least three elevation zones such as low area, hill, ridge, valley, or platform.
- Use Smooth to reduce harsh artifacts or unrealistic bumps.
- Use Flatten to create one intentional playable or focal area.
- Check the scene from a wide camera angle and a closer terrain angle.
- Upload evidence screenshots to Google Drive and paste links into DCC Creative Studio.
- Write a reflection naming the landscape tools you used.
