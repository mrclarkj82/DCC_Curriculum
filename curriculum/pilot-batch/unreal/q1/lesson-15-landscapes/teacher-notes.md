# Landscapes Teacher Notes

## Purpose of the Lesson

A strong environment starts with readable large shapes before small props, foliage, or decorations are added. This lesson gives students a practical production task connected to the transcript range 01:56:25-02:03:19.

## Source Alignment And Timestamp Note

This pilot lesson is aligned to the Unreal Engine 5 beginner tutorial transcript range 01:56:25-02:03:19. Covers creating a new map for landscapes, entering Landscape mode, creating a landscape, sculpting terrain, raising and lowering with mouse/Shift, brush size, falloff, smooth brush, and flatten brush for terraces or playable areas. Timestamp range is based on transcript search for the first complete landscape creation and sculpting explanation. Later environment-build ranges expand the same workflow.

## Teacher Mini-Lesson Talking Points

- Create or open a blank map and enter Landscape mode.
- Create the landscape using a manageable default setup.
- Show sculpting to raise terrain and Shift sculpting to lower it.
- Change brush size and strength so students see control levels.
- Use Smooth to repair a harsh sculpt.
- Use Flatten to create a terrace, path, or playable platform.
- Frame screenshots that prove wide form and close-up refinement.

## Likely Student Issues

- Students leave the landscape almost flat.
- Students sculpt spikes instead of believable hills or playable forms.
- Students forget to smooth or refine harsh edges.
- Students make terrain too large or too small to understand from screenshots.
- Students submit only a close-up and no wide shot.

## Intervention Ideas

- Ask students to point to the exact setting, object, or screenshot that proves the skill.
- Pair students for a 2-minute evidence check before submission.
- Have students reset to a simpler scene if visual clutter hides the target skill.
- For students who finish early, move them into the extension or studio challenge rather than unrelated features.

## Extension Ideas

Add a clear path, overlook, valley, or buildable platform that could support a future mini-world.

## Studio Challenge

Use terrain shape to guide the viewer toward one focal point without adding signs or labels.

## Vocabulary Emphasis

- Landscape: A terrain surface used to build outdoor ground in Unreal.
- Terrain: The shape and surface of outdoor ground such as hills, valleys, and cliffs.
- Sculpt: A landscape tool action that raises or lowers terrain.
- Smooth: A tool that softens sharp or bumpy terrain changes.
- Flatten: A tool that creates a more level area from a chosen height.
- Erosion: A natural wearing-down effect or terrain idea used to make landforms feel less artificial.
- Elevation: The height of terrain compared with nearby areas.
- Brush size: How wide the landscape tool affects the terrain.
- Brush strength: How strongly the tool changes terrain.
- Scale: How large the terrain feels compared with the player or objects.
