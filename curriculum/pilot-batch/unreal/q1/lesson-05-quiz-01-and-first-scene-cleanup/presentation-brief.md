## Big Idea

Cleanup day is like editing a rough draft before sharing it with an audience.

## Teacher-To-Student Instructional Framing

Frame the lesson as a real production day. Students are not just completing a task; they are collecting evidence that will help an audience understand how the project was made.

## Opening Hook

Show a messy starter scene and a cleaned-up version from the same camera angle. Ask which one gives stronger evidence of skill and why.

## Student-Friendly Analogy

Cleanup day is like editing a rough draft before sharing it with an audience.

## Why The Skill Matters

This skill helps students create stronger digital media, explain creative decisions, and gather process evidence for final reflection, critique, and portfolio use.

## Prerequisite Knowledge

- Basic project navigation, saving, and file organization.
- Ability to capture a screenshot or screen recording.
- Ability to upload evidence to Google Drive or YouTube and submit a link in DCC Creative Studio.
- Understanding that strong evidence shows process, not only final results.

## Likely Misconceptions

- Students may treat the quiz as the entire class period.
- Students may move objects randomly instead of improving readability.
- Students may submit screenshots without explaining tools or shortcuts used.

## Vocabulary Emphasis

- Review: A focused check of earlier skills and vocabulary so you know what is solid and what still needs practice.
- Feedback: Information from a teacher, peer, rubric, or self-check that helps you decide what to improve.
- Revision: A purposeful change that makes the work clearer, stronger, or more complete.
- Evidence: A screenshot, file, or written explanation that proves a skill was used.
- Portfolio screenshot: A polished image of work that could be used later to show growth or achievement.

## Demonstration Sequence

1. Review Quiz 1 workflow and expectations.
2. Open a Lesson 04 starter scene.
3. Find floating, intersecting, or confusing objects.
4. Use move, rotate, scale, focus, and camera framing to improve the scene.
5. Capture a stronger screenshot and write a short tool reflection.

## Student Work Expectations

1. Open the Q3 castle project and confirm the lesson target: I can show what I remember from Lessons 01-04 and improve my first Unreal scene using feedback, organization, and stronger evidence screenshots.
2. Complete or review Quiz 01 for Lessons 01-04.
3. Open the simple scene built in Lesson 04.
4. Clean up object placement.
5. Fix floating or intersecting objects.
6. Improve the camera angle for the final screenshot.
7. Organize visible evidence so the scene is easier to understand.
8. Take a stronger final screenshot.
9. Write a short reflection naming at least three tools or shortcuts used.
10. Capture daily screen recording or camera footage that shows the process.
11. Upload evidence to Google Drive or YouTube and set sharing so the teacher can view it.
12. Submit the approved evidence link in DCC Creative Studio with a short reflection.

## Intervention Options

- Reduce the task to one visible improvement and one evidence clip.
- Provide a sentence frame for the production log or reflection.
- Let students submit screenshots plus a written explanation if recording tools fail.
- Ask students to compare current evidence to the lesson target before continuing.
