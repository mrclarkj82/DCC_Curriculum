# Quiz 1 and First Scene Cleanup Teacher Notes

## Purpose of the Lesson

This checkpoint lesson gives students time to review Lessons 01-04, repair gaps, clean up their first scene, and submit stronger evidence before the class moves into exposure and materials.

## Suggested 90-Minute Block Schedule

- 0-7 minutes: Login, project setup, and bell ringer.
- 7-17 minutes: Teacher slides and success criteria.
- 17-35 minutes: Video/demo segment.
- 35-75 minutes: Unreal build task with teacher check-ins.
- 75-85 minutes: Submission evidence and file naming.
- 85-90 minutes: Exit ticket, save, and cleanup.

## Teacher Mini-Lesson Talking Points

- Frame Quiz 01 as a feedback tool, not only a grade.
- Model how to choose a cleanup target from a screenshot.
- Show a quick example of floating, intersecting, or confusing object placement.
- Remind students that evidence should prove a specific improvement.

## Likely Student Issues

- Submitting only a random screenshot instead of evidence of improvement.
- Changing too many objects without explaining what changed.
- Leaving floating or intersecting objects in the final scene.
- Writing a reflection that does not name tools or shortcuts used.

## Intervention Ideas

- Ask students to point to the exact setting, node, object, or screenshot that proves the skill.
- Pair students for a 2-minute evidence check before submission.
- For students who finish early, move them into the extension challenge rather than adding unrelated features.

## Extension Ideas

Create one portfolio-ready screenshot with a clear camera angle, readable object placement, and no distracting editor clutter.

## Vocabulary Emphasis

- Review: A focused check of earlier skills and vocabulary so you know what is solid and what still needs practice.
- Feedback: Information from a teacher, peer, rubric, or self-check that helps you decide what to improve.
- Revision: A purposeful change that makes the work clearer, stronger, or more complete.
- Evidence: A screenshot, file, or written explanation that proves a skill was used.
- Portfolio screenshot: A polished image of work that could be used later to show growth or achievement.

## What Evidence To Check Before Students Leave

- Before screenshot, if available.
- After screenshot.
- Short reflection naming at least three tools or shortcuts.
- Optional quiz completion screenshot or teacher checkoff.
