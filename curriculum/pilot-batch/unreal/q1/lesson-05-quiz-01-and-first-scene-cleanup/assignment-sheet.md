# First Scene Cleanup and Evidence Upgrade Assignment Sheet

## Objective

I can show what I remember from Lessons 01-04 and improve my first Unreal scene using feedback, organization, and stronger evidence screenshots.

## Skill Focus

- Quiz feedback helps identify what needs review
- Viewport navigation helps check the scene from useful angles
- Transform tools fix placement, scale, and rotation issues
- The Outliner helps select, organize, and rename visible objects
- Screenshots document evidence for teacher review

## Required Steps

1. Complete or review Quiz 01 for Lessons 01-04.
2. Open the simple scene built in Lesson 04.
3. Clean up object placement.
4. Fix floating or intersecting objects.
5. Improve the camera angle for the final screenshot.
6. Organize visible evidence so the scene is easier to understand.
7. Take a stronger final screenshot.
8. Write a short reflection naming at least three tools or shortcuts used.

## Naming Convention

Use this format when naming screenshots or exported evidence: LastName_FirstName_UE-Q1-A05_Description.

## Screenshot/File Evidence Requirements

- Before screenshot, if available.
- After screenshot.
- Short reflection naming at least three tools or shortcuts.
- Optional quiz completion screenshot or teacher checkoff.

## What To Do If Stuck

- Submitting only a random screenshot instead of evidence of improvement.
- Changing too many objects without explaining what changed.
- Leaving floating or intersecting objects in the final scene.
- Writing a reflection that does not name tools or shortcuts used.

## 4-Point Rubric

- 4: Complete, polished, on time, and clearly meets or extends the stated requirements.
- 3: Complete and meets the stated requirements.
- 2: Partially complete or missing required evidence.
- 1: Attempted but incomplete, unclear, or not functional.
- 0: Not submitted.

## Extension Challenge

Create one portfolio-ready screenshot with a clear camera angle, readable object placement, and no distracting editor clutter.

## Reflection Prompt

Name at least three Unreal tools or shortcuts you used, then explain how they improved the scene.
