## 7. A student walkthrough moves too fast and misses the important castle details. What should they revise?

- Question ID: ue-q3-quiz-04-q07
- Type: scenario
- Related lesson ID: ue-q3-l15
- Difficulty: medium
- Tag: troubleshooting

Correct answer: They should plan a slower route, pause at focal points, re-record from player view, and check that viewers can understand the scene.

Explanation: Walkthrough pacing should help viewers read the environment.

## 8. A final submission folder has great clips, but the teacher cannot open them. What is the likely issue and fix?

- Question ID: ue-q3-quiz-04-q08
- Type: scenario
- Related lesson ID: ue-q3-l16
- Difficulty: easy
- Tag: troubleshooting

Correct answer: The sharing permissions are likely restricted; the student should set Drive or YouTube sharing so the teacher can view the evidence before submitting.

Explanation: Link evidence only works when sharing permissions are correct.

## 9. Put the final evidence workflow in order: select evidence, organize folder, check sharing, write reflection, submit link.

- Question ID: ue-q3-quiz-04-q09
- Type: process-order
- Related lesson ID: ue-q3-l16
- Difficulty: medium
- Tag: workflow

Correct answer: 1. Select strongest evidence. 2. Organize the folder or links. 3. Check sharing permissions. 4. Write the final reflection. 5. Submit the link in DCC Creative Studio.

Explanation: Final submission needs organization and accessible links.
