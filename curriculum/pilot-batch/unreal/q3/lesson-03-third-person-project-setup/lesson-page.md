## Vocabulary

- Production log: A dated record of what was built, what was captured, what changed, and what needs to happen next.
- Screen recording: Video captured from the computer screen to show workflow, testing, or before/after changes.
- Behind-the-scenes footage: Camera or screen-recorded evidence that shows how a project was made.
- B-roll: Supporting footage that helps explain a process, place, detail, or action.

## Student Workflow

1. Open the Q3 castle project and confirm the lesson target: I can set up a playable third-person Unreal project and document why player scale matters for a castle walkthrough.
2. Set up or confirm a third-person project/template configuration.
3. Place or test the player character in the castle environment workspace.
4. Check player scale against temporary terrain or blockout objects.
5. Record a short walkthrough clip from the player perspective.
6. Update the production log with what the player test revealed.
7. Capture daily screen recording or camera footage that shows the process.
8. Upload evidence to Google Drive or YouTube and set sharing so the teacher can view it.
9. Submit the approved evidence link in DCC Creative Studio with a short reflection.
