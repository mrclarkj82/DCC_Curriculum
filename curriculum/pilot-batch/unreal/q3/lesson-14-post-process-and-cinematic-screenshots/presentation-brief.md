## Big Idea

The Q3 castle project is a movie set being built in public: the final scene matters, but the behind-the-scenes process tells the story.

## Teacher-To-Student Instructional Framing

Frame the lesson as a real production day. Students are not just completing a task; they are collecting evidence that will help an audience understand how the project was made.

## Opening Hook

Show a before/process/after view of post-process and cinematic screenshots and ask what the documentary audience would need to understand.

## Student-Friendly Analogy

The Q3 castle project is a movie set being built in public: the final scene matters, but the behind-the-scenes process tells the story.

## Why The Skill Matters

This skill helps students create stronger digital media, explain creative decisions, and gather process evidence for final reflection, critique, and portfolio use.

## Prerequisite Knowledge

- Basic project navigation, saving, and file organization.
- Ability to capture a screenshot or screen recording.
- Ability to upload evidence to Google Drive or YouTube and submit a link in DCC Creative Studio.
- Understanding that strong evidence shows process, not only final results.

## Likely Misconceptions

- Submitting only final screenshots with no process evidence.
- Recording too late, after the decision-making is already invisible.
- Forgetting to update the production log or Drive sharing permissions.

## Vocabulary Emphasis

- Production log: A dated record of what was built, what was captured, what changed, and what needs to happen next.
- Screen recording: Video captured from the computer screen to show workflow, testing, or before/after changes.
- Behind-the-scenes footage: Camera or screen-recorded evidence that shows how a project was made.
- B-roll: Supporting footage that helps explain a process, place, detail, or action.
- Castle environment: The Q3 Unreal scene students build, refine, walk through, and document.

## Demonstration Sequence

1. Choose a strong camera angle.
2. Check UI clutter, exposure, and framing.
3. Adjust post-process carefully.
4. Capture cinematic screenshots.
5. Log which shot could appear in the documentary.

## Formative Checks

- Students can state today's skill in their own words.
- Students can identify what process evidence they will capture before work time ends.
- Students can explain how the evidence supports the assignment or final project.

## Discussion Questions

- What would a viewer understand from your process evidence that they would miss in a final screenshot?
- Which decision today affects quality, mood, readability, or story?
- What evidence would prove that you did real production work today?

## Stop-And-Check Moments

- Before independent work: students name the evidence they plan to capture.
- Mid-work: teacher confirms at least one screenshot or recording exists.
- Before submission: students verify sharing and write the production log or reflection.

## Teacher Timing

- 0-7 minutes: Login, open project, bell ringer.
- 7-15 minutes: Hook, target, vocabulary, and evidence habit.
- 15-30 minutes: Teacher demo with one prediction question.
- 30-65 minutes: Independent production work.
- 65-78 minutes: Evidence capture, upload, and reflection.
- 78-86 minutes: DCC link submission.
- 86-90 minutes: Exit ticket, save, backup, cleanup.

## Student Work Expectations

1. Open the Q3 castle project and confirm the lesson target: I can use post-process and cinematic framing to capture polished castle visuals for the final documentary.
2. Set up or refine post-process settings for the castle environment.
3. Choose at least two cinematic camera angles.
4. Capture polished screenshots or short cinematic clips.
5. Record a short screen capture showing how one shot was composed.
6. Update the production log with which shot may appear in the final documentary.
7. Capture daily screen recording or camera footage that shows the process.
8. Upload evidence to Google Drive or YouTube and set sharing so the teacher can view it.
9. Submit the approved evidence link in DCC Creative Studio with a short reflection.

## Intervention Options

- Reduce the task to one visible improvement and one evidence clip.
- Provide a sentence frame for the production log or reflection.
- Let students submit screenshots plus a written explanation if recording tools fail.
- Ask students to compare current evidence to the lesson target before continuing.

## Extension Challenge

## Studio Challenge

## Reflection Support

Reflection prompt: What makes your strongest shot work?

## Exit Ticket
