## 1. What is the main job of landscape materials in a castle environment?

- Question ID: ue-q3-quiz-02-q01
- Type: multiple-choice
- Related lesson ID: ue-q3-l06
- Difficulty: easy
- Tag: landscape-materials

Choices:
- To make terrain zones readable and believable.
- To erase all landscape shapes.
- To replace the production log.
- To automatically build the castle.

Correct answer: To make terrain zones readable and believable.

Explanation: Materials help viewers understand paths, cliffs, grass, and other terrain surfaces.

## 7. A student downloads ten terrain assets but the scene looks cluttered and inconsistent. What should they do?

- Question ID: ue-q3-quiz-02-q07
- Type: scenario
- Related lesson ID: ue-q3-l07
- Difficulty: medium
- Tag: troubleshooting

Correct answer: They should choose fewer assets, check scale/style, place them with purpose, and document why each supports the castle environment.

Explanation: Asset use should support design rather than create random clutter.

## 8. A modular castle wall repeats the same piece so obviously that it looks artificial. What can improve it?

- Question ID: ue-q3-quiz-02-q08
- Type: scenario
- Related lesson ID: ue-q3-l08
- Difficulty: medium
- Tag: troubleshooting

Correct answer: The student can vary height, spacing, rotation, damage/detail pieces, surrounding props, or camera composition while keeping the structure organized.

Explanation: Variation makes modular construction feel more believable.

## 9. Put the modular castle workflow in order: review kit, block silhouette, assemble pieces, add variation, record progress.

- Question ID: ue-q3-quiz-02-q09
- Type: process-order
- Related lesson ID: ue-q3-l08
- Difficulty: medium
- Tag: workflow

Correct answer: 1. Review kit pieces. 2. Block the castle silhouette. 3. Assemble modular pieces. 4. Add variation. 5. Record progress evidence.

Explanation: The castle should move from broad readable form to more varied detail.
