# Teacher Notes: Landscape Materials

## Lesson Purpose

Students continue the Q3 castle environment project while capturing daily process evidence for a making-of documentary.

## Connection To Previous Lesson

Connect today's work to the previous production checkpoint by asking students what changed in the castle and what evidence already exists.

## Connection To Next Lesson

Students should leave with evidence that the next lesson can build on: screenshots, clips, production log notes, and one clear next-step decision.

## Suggested 90-Minute Block Schedule

- 0-7 minutes: Login, open DCC Creative Studio, open the Unreal project, answer bell ringer.
- 7-15 minutes: Review learning target, evidence expectations, and documentary capture habit.
- 15-30 minutes: Teacher mini-lesson and live demo.
- 30-65 minutes: Independent Unreal work with teacher check-ins.
- 65-78 minutes: Screen/camera recording, screenshot capture, and production log update.
- 78-86 minutes: Google Drive or YouTube link preparation and DCC submission.
- 86-90 minutes: Exit ticket, save project, backup, and cleanup.

## Teacher Talking Points

- The finished castle matters, but the documentary proves how the castle was made.
- Short daily recordings are more useful than one long recording at the end.
- Evidence should show decision-making, not just decoration.
- A clear production log protects students from forgetting what changed.

## Demo Sequence

1. Identify terrain zones that need different surfaces.
2. Apply or paint a landscape material change.
3. Check transitions from player view.
4. Record why one material belongs in the castle world.
5. Log the strongest material evidence.

## Likely Student Issues

- Submitting only final screenshots with no process evidence.
- Recording too late, after the decision-making is already invisible.
- Forgetting to update the production log or Drive sharing permissions.

## Intervention Strategies

- Give students a one-sentence evidence frame: "Today I changed ___ because ___, and my recording shows ___."
- Narrow the task to one visible improvement if a student is behind.
- Let students capture screenshots plus a written log if screen recording is unavailable.
- Pair a stuck student with a peer for a one-minute critique, then return to individual work.

## Extension Strategies

- Ask students to capture a before/after comparison from the same camera angle.
- Ask students to record a short voiceover explaining the production choice.
- Ask students to organize the clip for possible use in the final documentary.

## Vocabulary Emphasis

- Production log: A dated record of what was built, what was captured, what changed, and what needs to happen next.
- Screen recording: Video captured from the computer screen to show workflow, testing, or before/after changes.
- Behind-the-scenes footage: Camera or screen-recorded evidence that shows how a project was made.
- B-roll: Supporting footage that helps explain a process, place, detail, or action.
- Castle environment: The Q3 Unreal scene students build, refine, walk through, and document.

## Evidence To Verify Before Students Leave

- Before/after screenshots of landscape material changes.
- Daily documentary clip explaining one material choice.
- Production log entry for today.

## Technology / Setup Requirements

- Unreal Engine installed and accessible.
- Student Q3 project folder available.
- Screen recording or camera recording method available.
- Google Drive, Google Docs, or YouTube link submission access.
- DCC Creative Studio Today item or assignment page available.
