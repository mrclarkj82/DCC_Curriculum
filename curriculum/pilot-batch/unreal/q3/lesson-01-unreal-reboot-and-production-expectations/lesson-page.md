## Vocabulary

- Production log: A dated record of what was built, what was captured, what changed, and what needs to happen next.
- Screen recording: Video captured from the computer screen to show workflow, testing, or before/after changes.
- Behind-the-scenes footage: Camera or screen-recorded evidence that shows how a project was made.
- B-roll: Supporting footage that helps explain a process, place, detail, or action.
- Castle environment: The Q3 Unreal scene students build, refine, walk through, and document.

## Student Workflow

1. Open the Q3 castle project and confirm the lesson target: I can restart my Unreal workflow, set up my Q3 castle project, and begin documenting the production process with camera or screen-recorded evidence.
2. Create or open the assigned Q3 Unreal castle project.
3. Set up a clean project folder and naming convention for Unreal files and documentary evidence.
4. Write a short production goal for the castle environment.
5. Record a short screen capture or camera clip showing the project setup process.
6. Start a production log with the date, what you worked on, what you captured, and what you need next.
7. Capture daily screen recording or camera footage that shows the process.
8. Upload evidence to Google Drive or YouTube and set sharing so the teacher can view it.
9. Submit the approved evidence link in DCC Creative Studio with a short reflection.

## Common Problems and Fixes

- Problem: Submitting only final screenshots with no process evidence.
  Fix: Pause, check the lesson target, and capture evidence that directly shows the corrected workflow.
- Problem: Recording too late, after the decision-making is already invisible.
  Fix: Pause, check the lesson target, and capture evidence that directly shows the corrected workflow.
- Problem: Forgetting to update the production log or Drive sharing permissions.
  Fix: Pause, check the lesson target, and capture evidence that directly shows the corrected workflow.

## Student Help

- Save the Unreal project before recording evidence.
- Keep the Content Browser and Outliner organized as the castle grows.
- Record short clips often instead of waiting until the end of class.
- If recording fails, capture screenshots plus a written production log note before leaving.
- Ask whether the evidence shows process, decision-making, and final state.
